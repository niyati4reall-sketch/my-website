What is TalkYourMind?

A student-led HOSA mental health campaign focused on teen depression and anxiety.

  • Focus on teen depression & anxiety
  • Educate students and reduce stigma
  • Encourage open conversations
  • Promote mental health awareness

Purpose & Goal

Why we started and what we want to achieve.

The problem

Depression and anxiety are common in teens and impact academics and daily life. Many students are afraid to seek help due to lack of awareness and stigma.

Our goal

Educate, support, and connect students. TalkYourMind was developed through research and outreach to make a real difference.

What is depression?

Depression is more than just feeling sad—it's a serious mental health condition that affects how you think, feel, and act.

Common signs in teens

  • Persistent sadness or hopelessness
  • Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
  • Changes in sleep patterns (too much or too little)
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feeling tired or lacking energy
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide

Impact on daily life

Depression can make it hard to focus in school, maintain friendships, and enjoy everyday activities. It's not something you can just "snap out of"—it's a real medical condition that deserves attention and support.

Remember: You're not alone, and seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

What is anxiety?

Anxiety is a normal response to stress, but when it becomes overwhelming and persistent, it can interfere with daily life.

Common signs in teens

  • Excessive worry about everyday situations
  • Feeling restless or on edge
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat or sweating
  • Avoiding social situations or activities
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Feeling overwhelmed by school or responsibilities

Impact on daily life

Anxiety can make it difficult to participate in class, socialize with peers, or try new things. It might cause you to avoid situations that trigger worry, which can limit your experiences and growth.

Remember: Anxiety is treatable, and many effective strategies and resources are available to help you manage it.
