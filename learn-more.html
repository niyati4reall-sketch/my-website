Coping strategies and tips for talking about mental health.

Coping strategies

  • Stay active – Exercise, even a short walk, can improve mood and reduce anxiety.
  • Connect with others – Spend time with friends or family who make you feel supported.
  • Practice grounding – Focus on your senses (what you see, hear, feel) when you feel overwhelmed.
  • Get enough sleep – A regular sleep schedule helps both mental and physical health.
  • Limit screen time – Take breaks from social media and news if they add stress.
  • Try relaxation – Deep breathing, meditation, or journaling can help calm your mind.
  • Do things you enjoy – Hobbies and interests can give you a sense of purpose and calm.

How to talk about it

Choose someone you trust. A parent, teacher, counselor, or close friend can be a good place to start.

Pick a good time. Find a moment when you’re both relaxed and have time to talk without being interrupted.

Start small. You don’t have to share everything at once. “I’ve been struggling lately” can open the conversation.

Be honest. It’s okay to say you’re scared or don’t know how to put it into words.

Write it down. If speaking feels too hard, you can write a note or text to share how you feel.

Ask for what you need. “I just need someone to listen” or “I’d like help finding a counselor” can guide them.
